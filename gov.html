Pradhan Mantri Fasal Bima Yojana (PMFBY)

Launched: 18 February 2016

Benefits:

  • Provides comprehensive crop insurance against non-preventable natural risks like drought, floods, pests, and diseases.
  • Farmers pay a minimal premium (2% for Kharif crops, 1.5% for Rabi crops).
  • Encourages financial stability and reduces farmers' debt burden.

Kisan Credit Card (KCC) Scheme

Launched:1998 (by NABARD)

Benefits:

  • Provides farmers with easy access to short-term credit for agricultural needs.
  • Offers low-interest loans for purchasing seeds, fertilizers, and other inputs.
  • Includes insurance coverage against crop failure.
  • Reduces dependency on moneylenders and encourages financial inclusion.

e-NAM (National Agriculture Market)

Launched: 14 April 2016

Benefits:

  • A pan-India electronic trading platform for agricultural commodities.
  • Helps farmers get better prices by eliminating middlemen.
  • Enhances transparency and competition in the agricultural market.
  • Enables direct online transactions between buyers and sellers.

Rashtriya Krishi Vikas Yojana (RKVY)

Launched: 2007

Benefits:

  • Aims to promote agricultural growth and increase farmers' income.
  • Supports various initiatives like irrigation, seed production, and training.
  • Encourages states to develop region-specific agricultural strategies.
  • Boosts private investment in agricultural infrastructure.

Paramparagat Krishi Vikas Yojana (PKVY)

Launched: 2015

Benefits:

  • Promotes organic farming practices across India.
  • Encourages the use of traditional, eco-friendly farming techniques.
  • Provides financial assistance to farmers adopting organic farming.
  • Supports certification and marketing of organic products.

Soil Health Card Scheme

Launched: 19 February 2015

Benefits:

  • Helps farmers understand the nutrient status of their soil.
  • Recommends corrective measures to improve soil health and fertility.
  • Reduces excessive use of fertilizers and promotes balanced soil nutrition.
  • Enhances agricultural productivity through scientific soil management.
